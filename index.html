MakerLab

Supporting educational, social and technological innovation, the Brandeis MakerLab enables students, faculty, and staff to improve the world by creating things with their hands, hearts, and minds.

What we do

Our model supports four equipment categories:
1) tools that physicalize the digital world (3D printers, laser cutters, CNC)
2) tools that digitize the real world (3D scanning, computer vision)
3) hardware and software for virtual reality and augmented reality (HMD, touch, gesture, and multiscreen environments)
4) hardware and software for prototyping embedded systems and robotics

Teaching & Learning

Lectures in emerging technology, weekly trainings, and workshops.

Research Technology

Hardware and software support for instrument machines, prototyping, and research workflows.

Hackathons & Events

24 hour programming and prototyping challenges, speaker series, and sponsored Maker in Residence projects.

Outreach

Talks, tours, and building community.

Featured Projects

Graybiel Lab

"In my dissertation research I am studying how people maintain balance, and the role that the vision, the inner ear's balance organ, and gravity plays in that. We took some data from one of our experiments, and turned that into a 3d printed sculpture that represents that data in such a way that you can gain a different understanding from it, compared to "just" looking at a 2d graph on a screen. This was a collaboration with Vivekanand Pandey Vimal (who provided the data) and Alberto Pierobon (who helped with designing the stand) with a tremendous amount of support from Brandeis University LTS Maker Lab (Ian Roy, Hazal H Uzunkaya, Ben Levin) and Brandeis 3D Printing Club - Deis3D (Gabe Seltzer). " - Sacha Panic

Molecules of Life

This summer we had six Waltham High School students participate in a program (designed by Vivekanand Pandey Vimal, Professor Krummel, Anique Olivier-Mason and Eduardo Beltrame), where they learned about Biochemistry and DNA, with 3D printed molecules to help them visualize. You can read more about this program at Brandeis Library and Technology Services' blog.

Brandeis Prosthetics Club

A group of Brandeis students have started this club in order to understand and develop prostheses. The Prosthetics in the market can cost up to $50,000, by 3D printing we can reduce this price to $50. Now kids don't have to wait until they stop growing to get one, and this is very important in their neuro-muscular development. Right now the club members are trying to master the designs found in the e-NABLE community's website. Once they get certified they will put their first prosthetic hand on a kid. While they work on their 3D printing and design skills, they are also hoping to start strong relations with Boston Children's Hospital to help the children in need.
